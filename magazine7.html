Understanding Domestic Violence and Protection

認識家庭暴力與保護制度

英文文章原文

原文 Original Article

In July 2025, a horrific incident in Tucheng, New Taipei City, shocked the nation.

A man, reportedly angered by his wife's decision to prevent him from seeing their child and obtain a protection order against him, attacked both his wife and her sister in broad daylight, resulting in their deaths.

While such extreme cases make headlines, they represent only the visible tip of a much larger problem. Behind closed doors, countless individuals endure abuse in silence, their suffering often unnoticed until it is too late.

Domestic violence refers to patterns of abusive behavior used to control or harm someone in a close relationship. While it often occurs between spouses2 or partners, it may also involve other family or household members. It encompasses more than physical assault3. Emotional, financial, and sexual abuse are all common forms, each aiming to exert4 control over the victim. Emotional abuse may involve constant criticism, humiliation5, or threats that undermine6 a victim's self-esteem. Financial abuse occurs when one person controls all money or resources, leaving the other dependent and powerless. Sexual abuse, even within marriage, is also a form of domestic violence, as consent must never be assumed.

Identifying an abusive situation can be challenging, particularly when the abuse is subtle7 or gradual. Warning signs include a close family member or partner who monitors your every move, dictates8 who you can see, or belittles your achievements. You might feel anxious or fearful around them, walking on eggshells and constantly adjusting your actions to avoid triggering anger. Abusive behavior is often inconsistent. The abuser may behave kindly at times or promise to change, which can confuse the victim and obscure9 the seriousness of the abuse.

Taiwan's legal system addresses domestic violence through the Domestic Violence Prevention Act. This legislation1 allows victims to obtain protection orders that may prohibit the abuser from contacting or approaching them, require the abuser to move out of shared residences, and mandate2 participation in treatment programs. Police are obligated to respond immediately to domestic violence calls and may arrest perpetrators without warrants3 in certain cases. However, legal protection is only effective if victims know their rights and feel safe reporting abuse. Many hesitate due to fear, shame, or economic dependence, highlighting the need for ongoing public education and accessible resources.

Other countries offer valuable lessons in addressing domestic violence. In Spain, for example, risk assessment4 tools are employed to help authorities identify the cases most likely to escalate to lethal5 violence. "Clare's Law" in the UK allows individuals to request information about their partner's history of domestic violence. Specialized domestic violence courts in Australia and the US train judges to better understand victim trauma and patterns of control. At a more preventive6 level, Sweden focuses on education, teaching young people in schools about healthy relationships.

If you or someone you know is experiencing domestic violence, reaching out for help is the first step toward safety. Taiwan's 113 Protection Hotline offers confidential7 support and guidance 24 hours a day, providing immediate counseling and connections to emergency services, shelters, and legal assistance. Local family support centers offer practical help with housing8, employment, and childcare. When it's safe to do so, documenting9 evidence of abuse through photographs, medical records, or written accounts can help authorities respond more effectively. A clearer understanding of domestic violence and the resources available is crucial to reducing the risk of future tragedies.

八句精讀 8 Key Sentence Analyses

①事件背景與分詞結果

A man, reportedly angered by his wife's decision to prevent him from seeing their child and obtain a protection order against him, attacked both his wife and her sister in broad daylight, resulting in their deaths.

據報導，一名男子因妻子決定阻止他見孩子並取得保護令而憤怒，於光天化日下攻擊妻子與妻子的妹妹，導致兩人死亡。

文法解析：這句的主幹是 A man attacked both his wife and her sister。插入的 reportedly angered by... 是過去分詞片語，補充說明男子的狀態；句尾 resulting in their deaths 是現在分詞構句，表示前面攻擊行為造成的結果。
protection order：保護令in broad daylight：光天化日下result in：導致

②讓步子句與比喻

While such extreme cases make headlines, they represent only the visible tip of a much larger problem.

雖然這類極端案件會成為新聞頭條，但它們只代表一個更大問題中可見的一小部分。

文法解析：While... 在這裡不是「當……時」，而是讓步連接詞，意思是「雖然」。主句使用 the visible tip of a much larger problem 這個比喻，強調媒體看見的案件只是表層，背後仍有大量隱藏的受害者。
make headlines：登上新聞頭條visible tip：可見的一角

③關係子句修飾行為模式

Domestic violence refers to patterns of abusive behavior used to control or harm someone in a close relationship.

家庭暴力指的是在親密關係中，用來控制或傷害某人的虐待行為模式。

文法解析：used to control or harm... 是過去分詞片語，修飾前面的 patterns of abusive behavior，意思接近 that are used to...。其中 to control or harm 的 to 是不定詞，表示目的，不是介係詞。
refer to：指的是abusive behavior：虐待行為close relationship：親密關係

④分詞構句表示結果

Financial abuse occurs when one person controls all money or resources, leaving the other dependent and powerless.

當一個人控制所有金錢或資源，使另一方依賴且無力時，財務虐待便會發生。

文法解析：when one person controls... 是時間副詞子句，說明財務虐待發生的情境。句尾 leaving the other dependent and powerless 是現在分詞構句，表示前面動作帶來的結果，也就是被控制的一方變得依賴且無力。
financial abuse：財務虐待dependent：依賴的powerless：無力的

⑤關係子句與並列動詞

Warning signs include a close family member or partner who monitors your every move, dictates who you can see, or belittles your achievements.

警訊包括某位親近家人或伴侶會監控你的一舉一動、規定你可以見誰，或貶低你的成就。

文法解析：who monitors..., dictates..., or belittles... 是形容詞子句，修飾 a close family member or partner。三個動詞平行並列，呈現控制型關係常見的三種行為；who you can see 則是名詞子句，作為 dictates 的受詞。
warning signs：警訊dictate：支配；命令belittle：貶低

⑥法規權限與平行結構

This legislation allows victims to obtain protection orders that may prohibit the abuser from contacting or approaching them, require the abuser to move out of shared residences, and mandate participation in treatment programs.

這項法律允許被害人取得保護令，保護令可以禁止施暴者聯絡或接近被害人、要求施暴者搬離共同住所，並強制參與治療方案。

文法解析：主句是 This legislation allows victims to obtain protection orders，其中 to obtain 是受詞補語的不定詞。後面的 that may prohibit..., require..., and mandate... 是關係子句，三個動詞平行，清楚列出保護令可能具備的功能。
legislation：法律；立法prohibit A from V-ing：禁止 A 做某事shared residence：共同住所

⑦if 條件句與動名詞

However, legal protection is only effective if victims know their rights and feel safe reporting abuse.

然而，只有在被害人知道自己的權利，並且覺得通報虐待是安全的情況下，法律保護才有效。

文法解析：if victims know... 是條件副詞子句，說明法律保護有效的條件。feel safe reporting abuse 中的 reporting 是動名詞，表示「在通報虐待這件事上感到安全」。only effective if 讓語氣更強，提醒讀者制度與實際可近性同樣重要。
legal protection：法律保護report abuse：通報虐待

⑧when 子句與主詞動名詞

When it's safe to do so, documenting evidence of abuse through photographs, medical records, or written accounts can help authorities respond more effectively.

在安全的情況下，透過照片、醫療紀錄或書面記錄來保存虐待證據，可以幫助主管機關更有效地回應。

文法解析：When it's safe to do so 是時間條件子句，提醒行動前要先確認安全。主詞是很長的動名詞片語 documenting evidence of abuse...，動詞是 can help；help 後接原形動詞 respond，表示「幫助某人做某事」。
document evidence：記錄證據written accounts：書面記錄effectively：有效地

一、分詞構句表示補充結果

【引句 Quote】

Financial abuse occurs when one person controls all money or resources, leaving the other dependent and powerless.

【句型】

S + V ..., V-ing + O/C.

【核心說明】

現在分詞構句可以用來補充主句所造成的自然結果。它常放在句尾，讓句子更精簡，也讓讀者看出前後動作的因果關係。

本句中，控制金錢與資源是主句動作，而「使另一方依賴且無力」是結果。英文用 leaving... 連接，避免重複寫成一個完整句子。

【使用規則】

  • 分詞構句的邏輯主詞通常要和主句主詞或整個主句有清楚關係。
  • V-ing 可表示同時發生、附帶狀況或結果。
  • 若表示被動，常用 past participle，例如 angered by...。
  • 不要讓分詞片語的主詞不明，否則句意會混亂。

【主題例句】

Constant threats can isolate victims, making it harder for them to seek help.
（持續威脅可能孤立被害人，使他們更難求助。）

【學習提醒】

中文常用「然後、因此、使得」連接結果；英文若兩個動作關係密切，可以用 V-ing 簡化。寫作時要先確認主句完整，再加分詞構句。

【⚠️ 常見錯誤 / 🎯 精選試題】

✗ Controlling all money, the victim becomes powerless.（意思變成被害人控制金錢。）
✓ The abuser controls all money, leaving the victim powerless.（分詞構句清楚表示結果。）

選擇：The policy improved reporting systems, ___ victims faster access to help.
(A) gives (B) giving (C) gave
答案：B

二、關係子句修飾名詞

【引句 Quote】

Warning signs include a close family member or partner who monitors your every move, dictates who you can see, or belittles your achievements.

【句型】

N + who/that/which + V ...

【核心說明】

關係子句用來補充或限定前面的名詞。本句的 who 指人，修飾 a close family member or partner，說明這個人具有哪些控制行為。

同一個關係子句中可以放入平行動詞，例如 monitors、dictates、belittles。這種寫法能讓句子有層次，也能避免連續寫好幾個短句。

【使用規則】

  • 修飾人時常用 who 或 that；修飾事物時常用 which 或 that。
  • 關係子句要緊接被修飾的名詞，避免修飾錯對象。
  • 平行結構中的動詞形式要一致。
  • 非限定關係子句前要加逗號，補充額外資訊。

【主題例句】

A hotline that offers confidential guidance can help victims take the first step.
（提供保密指引的專線能幫助被害人踏出第一步。）

【學習提醒】

看到 who、which、that 時，先往前找它修飾的名詞，再判斷子句是否缺主詞或受詞。這能幫你拆解學測長句。

【⚠️ 常見錯誤 / 🎯 精選試題】

✗ People which need help should call the hotline.
✓ People who need help should call the hotline.（people 是人，要用 who。）

改錯：The law which protect victims is important.
答案：which protect → which protects

三、if 條件子句

【引句 Quote】

Legal protection is only effective if victims know their rights and feel safe reporting abuse.

【句型】

S + V + if + S + V ...

【核心說明】

if 子句用來說明條件：只有條件成立，主句的結果才可能成立。本句強調法律本身不夠，還需要被害人知道權利並能安全通報。

在真實條件句中，if 子句通常用現在式表示一般情況或未來可能性；主要子句可用現在式、未來式或情態助動詞。

【使用規則】

  • if 子句放句首時，後面通常加逗號；放句尾時通常不加逗號。
  • 真實條件句：If + S + 現在式, S + will/can/may + V。
  • 條件句可搭配 only，強調必要條件。
  • 不要在 if 子句中誤用 will 表示單純未來。

【主題例句】

If schools teach healthy relationships early, young people may recognize warning signs sooner.
（如果學校及早教導健康關係，年輕人可能更早辨識警訊。）

【學習提醒】

中文常說「如果……就……」，英文不一定要翻出 then。考試時若看到 if，先抓條件，再抓結果，長句就會清楚很多。

【⚠️ 常見錯誤 / 🎯 精選試題】

✗ If victims will know their rights, they can seek help.
✓ If victims know their rights, they can seek help.（真實條件句的 if 子句用現在式。）

選擇：Legal support can work better if people ___ how to report abuse.
(A) know (B) will know (C) knew
答案：A

練文筆食材⌄

① While + S + V, S + V（雖然……但是……）
→ 適用：先承認表面現象，再提出核心問題。
例：While punishment is necessary, prevention is even more important.

② the visible tip of a much larger problem（更大問題中可見的一角）
→ 適用：討論社會議題的隱藏面。
例：Online scams are only the visible tip of a much larger problem.

③ refer to + N（指的是……）
→ 適用：下定義。
例：Digital literacy refers to the ability to judge online information wisely.

④ be used to + V（被用來……）
→ 適用：說明工具或行為目的；to 是不定詞。
例：Social media can be used to spread helpful information.

⑤ more than + N（不只是……）
→ 適用：擴大讀者對概念的理解。
例：Education is more than memorizing facts.

⑥ each aiming to + V（每一個都旨在……）
→ 適用：列舉多個項目後總結共同目的。
例：The programs offer counseling and training, each aiming to rebuild confidence.

⑦ when + S + V, S + V（當……時）
→ 適用：說明情境與結果。
例：When pressure becomes too heavy, students should ask for help.

⑧ who + V, V, or V（關係子句平行動詞）
→ 適用：描述某人的多個特徵或行為。
例：A good leader listens carefully, explains clearly, and acts responsibly.

⑨ which can + V（這可能會……）
→ 適用：補充前一句造成的影響。
例：Rumors spread quickly online, which can damage a person's reputation.

⑩ through + N（透過……）
→ 適用：說明方法或途徑。
例：Communities can reduce fear through education and support.

⑪ the first step toward + N（邁向……的第一步）
→ 適用：寫結論或倡議行動。
例：Admitting a problem is the first step toward change.

⑫ help + O + V（幫助某人做某事）
→ 適用：說明政策、工具或行動的效果。
例：Clear rules help students understand their responsibilities.

⑬ be crucial to + V-ing/N（對……至關重要）
→ 適用：強調必要性。
例：Public trust is crucial to building a safer society.

⑭ reduce the risk of + N/V-ing（降低……的風險）
→ 適用：討論預防與公共安全。
例：Regular exercise can reduce the risk of serious illness.

⑮ not... until it is too late（直到太晚才……）
→ 適用：提醒忽視問題的後果。
例：Many people do not value clean water until it is too late.
