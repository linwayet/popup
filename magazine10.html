原文 Original Article

Farting away the pain, a step toward comfort. A fart walk may sound silly, but it refers to a surprisingly effective and practical activity, taking a short walk to relieve digestive discomfort. Though not a medical term, the phrase has gained popularity as a simple way to describe post-meal walking. Behind its playful name actually lies a concept backed by scientific evidence. Gentle walking stimulates the digestive system and helps trapped gas move more easily through the intestines. This process reduces bloating and the tight, uncomfortable feeling many people experience after a heavy meal.

In addition to its digestive benefits, fart walks also promote cardiovascular health. Regular walking improves blood circulation and keeps blood vessels flexible. This, in turn, helps lower blood pressure and reduces the risk of heart disease. Walking after meals also helps regulate blood sugar levels, particularly after carbohydrate-rich meals.

Physical activity encourages muscles to absorb glucose more efficiently, improving the body's ability to manage sugar. Even brief walks can make a difference and are especially beneficial for those concerned about post-meal blood sugar spikes. Over time, this simple routine can increase cardiovascular fitness, strengthen muscles and bones, boost energy, and improve sleep quality.

To get the most benefits from a fart walk, both timing and diet matter. Ideally, start walking 30 to 60 minutes after eating, when blood sugar levels typically peak.

What you eat also plays a role. While post-meal walks help support stable blood sugar levels, preventing sharp spikes begins with balanced meals. Choosing whole, fiber-rich food slows the absorption of glucose and reduces sudden increases in blood sugar. Still, fart walks are not a cure-all. Chronic stress can interfere with digestion, and persistent bloating may indicate an underlying condition that walking alone cannot resolve. Listening to your body and seeking medical advice when needed are still important parts of staying healthy.

一、不定詞表目的

【引句 Quote】

A fart walk refers to taking a short walk to relieve digestive discomfort.

【句型】

S + V + O + to V

【核心說明】

不定詞 to V 常用來說明動作的目的，相當於中文「為了……」。在本句中，to relieve digestive discomfort 說明「短暫散步」的目的。

to 在這裡是不定詞符號，不是介係詞，所以後面接原形動詞 relieve，而不是 relieving。

【使用規則】
  • 最常見形式是 to + 原形動詞。
  • 較正式時可用 in order to + V 或 so as to + V。
  • 否定目的常用 in order not to + V 或 so as not to + V。
  • to V 可放在句中修飾名詞，也可放句尾說明目的。
【主題例句】

Many students take a short walk after dinner to clear their minds.
（許多學生晚餐後短暫散步，為了讓頭腦清醒。）

【學習提醒】

看到 to 不要立刻判斷為介係詞。若後面接原形動詞，就是不定詞；若後面接名詞或 V-ing，才可能是介係詞用法。

【⚠️ 常見錯誤 / 🎯 精選試題】

✗ She walks after meals to relieving discomfort.
✓ She walks after meals to relieve discomfort.（to 是不定詞，後面接原形動詞。）

選擇題：He drank water ______ stay awake during class.
A. to　B. for　C. with
答案：A

二、分詞構句表結果

【引句 Quote】

Physical activity encourages muscles to absorb glucose more efficiently, improving the body's ability to manage sugar.

【句型】

S + V ..., V-ing ...

【核心說明】

逗號後的 V-ing 可以形成分詞構句，用來補充主要子句的結果、原因、時間或附帶狀況。本句中的 improving 說明前面「肌肉更有效率吸收葡萄糖」所帶來的結果。

這種寫法常見於科普或說明文，能讓句子更緊湊，也避免一直使用 and it。

【使用規則】
  • 現在分詞 V-ing 常表示主動或同時發生。
  • 若表示結果，通常放在主要子句後方並用逗號隔開。
  • 分詞構句的邏輯主詞要能從主要子句清楚判斷。
  • 若動作是被動，可使用過去分詞開頭。
【主題例句】

Regular walking improves circulation, reducing the risk of certain health problems.
（規律步行改善循環，進而降低某些健康問題的風險。）

【學習提醒】

分詞構句不是隨便把第二句改成 V-ing。改寫時要確認前後邏輯合理，且讀者能知道 V-ing 的動作或結果和誰有關。

【⚠️ 常見錯誤 / 🎯 精選試題】

✗ Walking after meals helps blood sugar, improve health.
✓ Walking after meals helps blood sugar, improving health.（逗號後用 V-ing 補充結果。）

改錯題：Gentle exercise supports digestion, reduce bloating.
答案：Gentle exercise supports digestion, reducing bloating.

三、原因副詞子句

【引句 Quote】

While post-meal walks help support stable blood sugar levels, preventing sharp spikes begins with balanced meals.

【句型】

Although / Though / While + S + V, S + V

【核心說明】

while 在這句不是「當……的時候」，而是讓步或對比用法，接近「雖然」。它先承認餐後散步有幫助，再轉到更核心的飲食平衡。

文章中也有 because、when 等副詞子句，這些連接詞能清楚標示原因、時間或讓步關係，是閱讀長句時的重要路標。

【使用規則】
  • because 表示明確原因，語氣最直接。
  • when 表示時間，說明動作發生的時機。
  • while 可表示「當……時」，也可表示「雖然」。
  • 副詞子句放句首時，後面通常加逗號。
  • because of 是介係詞片語，後面接名詞，不接完整子句。
【主題例句】

Because balanced meals slow glucose absorption, they help prevent sudden blood sugar spikes.
（因為均衡飲食會減緩葡萄糖吸收，所以能幫助預防血糖突然飆升。）

【學習提醒】

不要把 while 每次都翻成「當……時」。如果前後句有對比或讓步，while 常應理解成「雖然；儘管」。

【⚠️ 常見錯誤 / 🎯 精選試題】

✗ Because of chronic stress can affect digestion, rest is important.
✓ Because chronic stress can affect digestion, rest is important.（because 後接子句；because of 後接名詞。）

選擇題：______ walking helps, balanced meals are still necessary.
A. While　B. Because of　C. During
答案：A

練文筆食材⌄
① refer to N as N（把……稱為……）
→ 適用：介紹新名詞或概念時
例：People refer to this habit as a simple way to support digestion.
② Though + reduced clause（雖然……）
→ 適用：用精簡方式做讓步開頭
例：Though not a cure-all, walking after meals can be helpful.
③ Behind N lies N（在……背後有……）
→ 適用：讓句子更有書面感，強調隱藏原因
例：Behind this simple habit lies a useful health concept.
④ help + O + V（幫助……做……）
→ 適用：說明某方法帶來的具體作用
例：Gentle movement helps the body manage sugar more effectively.
⑤ in addition to + N（除了……之外）
→ 適用：補充第二個優點
例：In addition to improving mood, walking supports heart health.
⑥ This, in turn, V（這進而……）
→ 適用：寫因果鏈
例：Exercise improves circulation. This, in turn, helps reduce health risks.
⑦ be particularly beneficial for N（對……特別有益）
→ 適用：精準指出受益族群
例：This routine is particularly beneficial for people with busy schedules.
⑧ Over time, S + V（長期下來……）
→ 適用：描述累積效果
例：Over time, small habits can lead to meaningful changes.
⑨ both A and B（A 和 B 兩者都）
→ 適用：並列兩個重點
例：Both timing and diet matter when we try to stay healthy.
⑩ when S + V（當……時）
→ 適用：說明最佳時機
例：Start reviewing vocabulary when your mind is still fresh.
⑪ While S + V, S + V（雖然……但是……）
→ 適用：承認一點後轉入主論點
例：While exercise helps, balanced meals are still important.
⑫ begin with + N/V-ing（從……開始）
→ 適用：提出解方的起點
例：Preventing stress begins with a regular sleep schedule.
⑬ may indicate N（可能顯示……）
→ 適用：描述徵兆與可能原因
例：A sudden drop in grades may indicate a deeper learning problem.
⑭ cannot V alone（不能單靠……）
→ 適用：避免過度簡化問題
例：Technology cannot solve educational problems alone.
⑮ Listening to N and V-ing...（聆聽……並……）
→ 適用：文章結尾提出平衡建議
例：Listening to your body and resting when needed are part of a healthy routine.
