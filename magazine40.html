The Evolution of the Hula Hoop

呼拉圈的前世今生：從古代遊戲到現代狂熱

林威老師與 AI agent 團隊製作

【區塊一】英文文章原文

【原文 Original Article】

The Hula Hoop is a hoop-shaped toy, typically a hollow plastic tube, kept revolving around the waist by swiveling the hips. Its name comes from the hula, a traditional Hawaiian dance that features a similar rhythmic hip motion. Although the idea of spinning a hoop around the body is far from new — ancient cultures in Egypt and Greece used hoops for exercise and play as early as 500 BC — the modern version that millions of people recognize today has a much more recent origin.

The story of the modern Hula Hoop begins in Australia during the 1950s. Australian businessman Alex Tolmer, founder of the toy company Toltoys, was the first person to manufacture a hoop from plastic — a lightweight yet durable material that was relatively new at the time. Recognizing that children already loved playing with bamboo hoops, Tolmer switched to high-density polyethylene, a stronger form of plastic that made the toy both affordable and long-lasting. His company sold nearly half a million hoops across Australia, proving there was enormous public demand for the product.

The hoop's success soon caught the attention of American entrepreneurs Richard Knerr and Arthur Melin, founders of the toy company Wham-O. After purchasing the American rights, they launched the Hula Hoop in southern California in 1958, using a newly developed plastic called Marlex. The response was immediate and overwhelming — 25 million hoops were sold within just a few months, and total sales exceeded 100 million units in the United States within two years. The toy rapidly became a nationwide craze and an enduring symbol of 1950s youth culture.

【區塊四】文法解析

【文法解析 Grammar Analysis】

【文法名稱】Relative Clause 形容詞子句：限定 vs 非限定

【引句 Quote】

... the modern version that millions of people recognize today has a much more recent origin.

【句型】

N + that / who / which + S + V ...

【核心說明】

形容詞子句用來修飾前面的名詞（先行詞）。分為「限定子句」（無逗號）與「非限定子句」（有逗號）。限定子句提供必要資訊以區別先行詞；非限定子句則提供額外補充資訊。

【使用規則】

  • 限定用法：不加逗號。The toy that I bought is broken.（我買的那款玩具壞了。）
  • 非限定用法：加逗號。My brother, who lives in Australia, loves surfing.（我哥哥——他住在澳洲——熱愛衝浪。）
  • 非限定子句不可使用 that，只能用 who 或 which。
  • which 也可修飾前面整件事。

【主題例句】

Toltoys, which was founded by Alex Tolmer, was a major toy company in Australia.
（由艾力克斯·托爾默創辦的 Toltoys，曾是澳洲的一家大型玩具公司。）

【學習提醒】

在寫作時，若先行詞是獨一無二的專有名詞（如 Wham-O），其後的形容詞子句通常為非限定用法，須加逗號。

【⚠️ 常見錯誤 / 🎯 精選試題】

✗ Wham-O, that launched the Hula Hoop, is a famous company.

✓ Wham-O, which launched the Hula Hoop, is a famous company.（逗號後不可用 that）

選擇題：The student ________ won the competition is my cousin. (A) which (B) that (C) what (D) whom
答案：B。說明：先行詞是人，限定用法可用 that 或 who。

【文法名稱】Participle Construction 分詞構句

【引句 Quote】

Recognizing that children already loved playing with bamboo hoops, Tolmer switched to high-density polyethylene...

【句型】

V-ing ..., S + V ... / S + V ..., V-ing ...

【核心說明】

分詞構句是將副詞子句（如 because, when, after）簡化的結果。當副詞子句主詞與主句主詞相同時，可省略連接詞與主詞，並將動詞改為分詞形式（主動 V-ing，被動 V-ed）。

【使用規則】

  • 主動關係：用現在分詞 V-ing。Hearing the news, she cried.
  • 被動關係：用過去分詞 V-ed。Born in 1950, he grew up during the Hula Hoop craze.
  • 表同時發生的動作或附帶狀況。
  • 表連續發生的動作或結果。

【主題例句】

Switching to plastic, Tolmer made the hoop more durable and affordable.
（改用塑膠後，托爾默使圈圈變得更耐用且平價。）

【學習提醒】

使用分詞構句時，一定要檢查分詞的邏輯主詞是否與主句主詞一致，否則會造成「垂懸分詞」的錯誤。

【⚠️ 常見錯誤 / 🎯 精選試題】

✗ Recognizing the demand, 25 million hoops were sold.（賣掉的是圈圈，並非圈圈「意識到」需求）

✓ Recognizing the demand, Wham-O sold 25 million hoops.

改錯題：Hearing the bell, the classroom became quiet. (修正主詞不一致)
答案：Hearing the bell, the students became quiet.

【文法名稱】Passive Voice 被動語態：各時態用法

【引句 Quote】

25 million hoops were sold within just a few months...

【句型】

S + be + V-ed (p.p.) (+ by ...)

【核心說明】

被動語態用於強調受詞（接受動作的人或物），而非執行者。其結構隨時態而改變 be 動詞的形式，但主要動詞永遠是過去分詞 p.p.。

【使用規則】

  • 現在簡單：is/are + p.p. (Hoops are used for exercise.)
  • 過去簡單：was/were + p.p. (The toy was launched in 1958.)
  • 現在完成：have/has been + p.p. (Millions of hoops have been produced.)
  • 助動詞：will be / can be + p.p. (The record will be broken.)

【主題例句】

The Hula Hoop was originally manufactured in Australia using plastic.
（呼拉圈最初是在澳洲使用塑膠製造的。）

【學習提醒】

不及物動詞（如 happen, appear, occur）沒有被動語態，不可說 was appeared。

【⚠️ 常見錯誤 / 🎯 精選試題】

✗ The toy was appeared in the 1950s.

✓ The toy appeared in the 1950s.

翻譯題：這項產品正在被世界各地的人們使用。
答案：The product is being used by people all over the world.

🍳 練文筆食材▼

① Far from + adj.（一點也不……）
→ 適用：用來反駁一般的看法或預期。
例：The problem is far from simple.（這個問題一點也不簡單。）

② Recognizing that + S + V（意識到……）
→ 適用：描述採取某項行動的前導思考。
例：Recognizing that time was running out, he made a quick decision.

③ Switch to + N（轉向…… / 改用……）
→ 適用：描述策略或資源的改變。
例：Many companies are switching to renewable energy.

④ Exceed one's expectations（超過某人的預期）
→ 適用：描述極大的成功或意外的好結果。
例：The quality of his work exceeded my expectations.

⑤ An enduring symbol of ...（……的不朽象徵）
→ 適用：描述具有長期歷史意義的人或物。
例：The eagle is an enduring symbol of freedom.

⑥ Prove that + S + V（證明……）
→ 適用：引出實驗、數據或事件的結論。
例：The test results prove that the new medicine is effective.

⑦ Total ... exceeded ...（總數超過了……）
→ 適用：描述數量的巨大增長。
例：Total users exceeded one million last month.

⑧ Catch the attention of ...（引起……的注意）
→ 適用：描述某事變得引人注目。
例：His talent soon caught the attention of famous coaches.

⑨ Within + [time]（在……時間內）
→ 適用：強調動作完成的迅速。
例：The project was finished within three weeks.

⑩ A nationwide craze（全國性的狂熱）
→ 適用：描述某種極受歡迎的社會現象。
例：Social media has become a nationwide craze.
