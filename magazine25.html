Safety in Public Spaces: Be Ready for the Unexpected

公共場所安全：隨時準備應對突發狀況
林威老師與 AI agent 團隊製作

【區塊一】英文文章原文

【原文 Original Article】

In December 2025, a series of attacks occurred at Taipei MRT stations. The attacker used smoke bombs before starting random knife attacks. Many people were at first confused by the smoke, but these few seconds of confusion are often the key time to escape. An important rule to protect yourself is "run, hide, fight."

"Run" means to get away as fast as you can. Don't "freeze" to confirm what's happening. As you escape, warn others nearby and call 110. "Hide" means to stay out of the attacker's sight, find cover, and stay quiet. If you can't escape, hide behind solid barriers, such as counters, or lock yourself inside a room. Turn off your devices, stay low, and keep your body protected. "Fight" only when all else fails and you have no choice but to resist. The key idea is not winning, but creating an opening to escape.

Finally, remember your environment is full of tools. A backpack can shield your torso. A jacket wrapped around your arm can protect you. An umbrella or even a chair can help create distance or cover while you move away.

Taiwan's public transportation system and event venues offer reliable safety tools. For passengers, it helps to know what support already exists and how to use it quickly.

Take the Taiwan High Speed Rail as an example. Both emergency buttons and intercom systems are available on trains. These tools let passengers report suspicious activity, medical emergencies, or violent events in real time. In addition, there is a hidden safety feature: The seat cushions can be removed in emergencies and used as protective padding. To keep everyone safe, the company also works closely with the police, using CCTV cameras and security control rooms to watch the environment. At major stations, you may notice more security guards and staff during peak hours.

Whether you are on a train or at a large concert, the way to stay safe is the same: stay alert and know your surroundings. At sports games and festival events, organizers also have safety plans in place. When you arrive, take a minute to look around for the nearest exits. Notice where the staff members are so that you can find them quickly if you need help or directions.

While we must stay alert in public, we also need to have the right supplies ready. A simple step is keeping a grab-and-go kit. This kit is designed for quick evacuation. It should include a bottle of water, face masks, wet wipes, as well as a power bank and cables to stay connected. Don't forget to pack a first aid kit, a whistle, and a flashlight, along with cash and copies of your IDs. Keep the kit light, and check it at least twice a year.

However, tools alone are not enough; you also need some life-saving skills. In a crisis, your top priority is to keep yourself safe so you don't become another victim. Once you're safe, call emergency services and then check for the most serious injuries. For heavy bleeding, apply firm, direct pressure on the wound; if available, pack the wound with gauze and keep pressure on. For serious bleeding from an arm or a leg, a tourniquet can be used when pressure isn't enough. If many people are hurt, focus on helping those with life-threatening injuries first while waiting for emergency services.

There is nothing more powerful than staying clear-headed in a crisis—remembering to run, hide, or fight, and acting without hesitation.

【區塊四】文法解析

【文法解析 Grammar Analysis】

1. 現在分詞構句 (Participle Phrase)

...the company also works closely with the police, using CCTV cameras and security control rooms...
S + V ..., V-ing ...

核心說明：當兩個動作同時發生，且主詞相同時，可以將其中一個動作簡化為分詞構句。V-ing 通常用來補充說明動作的方式、手段或伴隨情況。

使用規則：

  • 主詞必須一致：省略從句的主詞與連接詞。
  • 主動關係：被簡化的動詞若與主詞為「主動」關係，改為 V-ing。
  • 位置靈活：可放在句中補充，或放在句尾說明結果。

主題例句：

  • Passengers waited for the train, checking their phones for updates.（乘客們等待火車，同時查看手機更新資訊。）
  • Organizers prepared for the event, setting up safety barriers around the stage.（主辦單位為活動做準備，在舞台周圍設置安全障礙物。）

學習提醒：分詞構句可以讓句子更簡潔。注意 V-ing 與主詞的邏輯關係必須是主動的。

✗ The company works with police, used CCTV cameras.
✓ The company works with police, using CCTV cameras.
說明：此處為伴隨動作，應使用現在分詞 using。
選擇題：The student ran out of the building, ________ for help.
(A) shout (B) shouted (C) shouting (D) to shout
答案：C。說明：現在分詞表伴隨動作。

2. 助動詞的被動語態 (Passive Voice with Modals)

The seat cushions can be removed in emergencies and used as protective padding.
Modal (can/should/must) + be + V-ed (p.p.)

核心說明：當動作的主體不重要或不需要強調時，使用被動語態。結合助動詞（如 can, should, must）時，結構為「助動詞 + be + 過去分詞」。

使用規則：

  • 結構固定：助動詞後接原形 be，再接 p.p.。
  • 多個動詞：若有多個被動動詞並列，後面的 be 常可省略，如本句中的 (can be) used。
  • 強調對象：重點在於「座墊」而非「誰拆卸它」。

主題例句：

  • Emergency buttons should be pressed only in a real crisis.（緊急按鈕應僅在真正的危機中按下。）
  • Safety tools must be checked regularly to ensure they work.（必須定期檢查安全工具以確保其運作正常。）

學習提醒：過去分詞 (p.p.) 的拼寫需注意規則與不規則變化。

✗ The kit can remove quickly.
✓ The kit can be removed quickly.
說明：包包是被移除的，需用被動語態。
選擇題：The emergency exits must ________ clear at all times.
(A) keep (B) be kept (C) kept (D) keeping
答案：B。說明：出口必須「被保持」通暢。

3. 不定詞表目的 (Infinitive for Purpose)

To keep everyone safe, the company also works closely with the police...
To + V ..., S + V ... / S + V ... to + V ...

核心說明：不定詞 (to + V) 最常見的用法之一是表示「目的」，相當於 in order to。放在句首時具有強調效果，且後面通常接逗號。

使用規則：

  • 語意轉化：可與 so that 子句互相替換（需調整結構）。
  • 否定用法：若要表達「為了不……」，應使用 in order not to 或 so as not to。
  • 位置：放在句首強調目的；放在句尾則語氣較自然。

主題例句：

  • People carry whistles to call for help in an emergency.（人們攜帶哨子以便在緊急情況下求救。）
  • To avoid confusion, the staff gave clear directions to the exit.（為了避免混亂，工作人員給出了前往出口的清晰指引。）

學習提醒：不要把表示目的的 to 與介係詞 to 搞混。

✗ For stay safe, we should stay alert.
✓ To stay safe, we should stay alert.
說明：表示目的應使用不定詞 to V 而非介係詞 for。
選擇題：He used a flashlight ________ the way in the dark.
(A) find (B) to finding (C) to find (D) found
答案：C。說明：不定詞 to V 表示目的。

折疊四：練文筆食材

① ...is the key time to...（……是……的關鍵時刻）
例：The first few hours are the key time to save the patients.

② ...as fast as you can（盡可能快地……）
例：You should finish the assignment as fast as you can.

③ ...only when... and you have no choice but to...（唯有當……且你別無選擇只能……）
例：Use the emergency exit only when there is a fire and you have no choice but to leave immediately.

④ Take ... as an example.（以……為例。）
例：Take the new policy as an example; it has many benefits for students.

⑤ Whether you are ..., the way to ... is the same: ...（無論你是在……，……的方法都是一樣的：……）
例：Whether you are a student or a teacher, the way to succeed is the same: stay diligent.

⑥ ...is designed for...（……是為……而設計的）
例：This building is designed for senior residents.

⑦ Don't forget to ..., along with ...（別忘了……，以及……）
例：Don't forget to bring your passport, along with the tickets.

⑧ ...so you don't become another victim.（……以便你不會成為另一個受害者。）
例：Follow the safety rules so you don't become another victim of the accident.

⑨ Once you're safe, ...（一旦你安全了，……）
例：Once you're safe, call your family to let them know.

⑩ There is nothing more powerful than ...（沒有什麼比……更強大了）
例：There is nothing more powerful than staying positive in difficult times.
