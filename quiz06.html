① 慣用語語意判斷　exacerbate = "add fuel to the fire" Q36 錯題

★ 來源：Q36（璇選 A「Broke the ice」，正解 D「Added fuel to the fire」）

❌ 璇的思路（為何選錯）
「broke the ice」也是常見慣用語，而且「ice」出現在文章情境裡⋯
✅ 正確判斷方式：先查語意，再對照上下文
add fuel to the fire　＝　火上加油（使事情更嚴重）→ 對應 exacerbate「使惡化」
break the ice　＝　打破僵局（開始對話或活動）→ 與「惡化」無關
cost an arm and a leg　＝　代價高昂 → 與語意無關
miss the boat　＝　錯過機會 → 與語意無關

🔑 解題心法：遇到慣用語替換題，先把四個選項都用「中文語意」對照，再看哪一個最接近原句動詞的意思。「exacerbate」= 使惡化 = 火上加油，不是「打破僵局」。

Freezing water rushed in and exacerbated the situation — it was like adding fuel to the fire.
冰冷的海水湧入，使情況更加惡化——就像火上加油一樣。
Her angry reply only added fuel to the fire and made the argument worse.
她憤怒的回應只是火上加油，讓爭吵更加激烈。
To break the ice at the party, he suggested playing a group game first.
為了在派對上打破僵局，他建議先玩一個團體遊戲。

② 篇章結構：段落邏輯 + 代名詞回指　Q33 錯題

★ 來源：Q33（璇選 D「genuine smile」，正解 A「Another crucial factor is immigration diversity」）

❌ 璇的思路（為什麼 D 看起來對）
「genuine smile」vs「forced smile」 聽起來很完整，像是一個對比說明的好句子⋯
✅ 正確判斷方式：看「空格後面」的句子，找關鍵詞線索
篇章結構題解題三步驟：
Step 1　讀空格前一句：找主題關鍵詞
Step 2　讀空格後一句：找「銜接詞」或「代名詞」
Step 3　確認哪個選項能同時承上啟下

Q33 情境：空格後句說「Newcomers from different parts of the world are found to smile more...」→ 關鍵詞是「移民」。只有 A「Another crucial factor is immigration diversity」能帶出「移民」這個主題。選項 D 說的是「真誠微笑 vs 假笑」，後面的句子完全沒有接這個對比，所以 D 邏輯不通。

A: Another crucial factor is a society's immigration diversity.
→ 後句："Newcomers from different parts of the world are found to smile more."
A：另一個關鍵因素是社會的移民多元性。→ 後句提到「移民」→ 完美銜接 ✅
D: A genuine smile indicates contentment, while a forced smile suggests underlying distress.
→ 後句："Newcomers from different parts of the world are found to smile more."
D：真誠的微笑代表滿足，假笑代表潛在痛苦。→ 後句跳到移民話題 → 邏輯斷裂 ❌

③ 細節推論：正確理解 "favored" 的方向　Q42 錯題

★ 來源：Q42（璇選 A「too narrow to allow any quick retreat」，正解 B「favored right-handed defenders」）

❌ 璇的思路（A 的陷阱）
文章確實說樓梯很「narrow」⋯ 但 A 說「too narrow to allow any quick retreat」，文章說的是 defenders 可以 retreat swiftly，所以 A 說「無法快速撤退」是錯的。
✅ B 說的是：順時針設計讓右撇子「防衛者」佔優勢
原文重點：
- attackers: right hand tight against the narrowest part → cannot use sword effectively
- defenders: familiar with stair pattern → can retreat up them very swiftly
→ 結論：B「favored right-handed castle defenders」正確 ✅

🔑 解題心法：推論題（infer）一定要回原文核對。選項 A 說「too narrow to allow any quick retreat」，但原文說守衛者能「swiftly retreat」—— 這是 A 的錯誤關鍵。閱讀時要注意 any / all / never / only 這類絕對用語，往往是干擾選項的設計點。

The defenders, familiar with the stair pattern, could retreat up them very swiftly.
守衛者對樓梯的規律熟悉，因此能夠非常迅速地向上撤退。（原文）
The clockwise design favored right-handed defenders by limiting the attackers' sword arm.
順時針的設計限制了攻擊者的持劍手臂，從而有利於右撇子守衛者。

④ 單字語意推論：a blip = a temporary state　Q45 錯題

★ 來源：Q45（璇選 D「an advanced system」，正解 A「a temporary state」）

"a blip" 字面義：雷達螢幕上短暫出現的光點
引申義：短暫的偏差；一時的例外（= a temporary irregularity）

上下文線索：
"...perhaps the north-on-top practice is less a rule and more a blip."
"...it seems we've ended up right where we began in ancient times..."
→ 全句意思：「北方朝上」不是永久規則，而是歷史上的短暫偏差

🔑 不認識生字怎麼辦？
Step 1：用上下文推斷：「less a rule and more a blip」→ blip 與 rule 相對 → blip 不是「規則」，而是「例外」
Step 2：看尾段主旨：「我們回到了古代的出發點」→ 暗示北方朝上只是一時的
Step 3：刪去法：B「urgent need」、C「critical decision」、D「advanced system」都與「短暫」無關 → 選 A

The north-on-top standard was perhaps a blip—a temporary state—not a fixed universal rule.
「北方朝上」的標準也許只是短暫的偏差，而非固定的普遍規則。
Don't worry; this low score is just a blip. You'll do better next time.

Against All Odds: The Story of Shackleton's Endurance

In 1914, Sir Ernest Shackleton set sail on a legendary voyage to Antarctica. His goal was to lead the first land crossing of the continent. Along with a skilled crew, he departed from Plymouth, UK, on a vessel called the Endurance. Little did he know that the expedition would soon face disaster.

Two days after leaving South Georgia Island, the ship encountered floating ice and was completely trapped. For nine months, the vessel drifted helplessly. Gradually, the pressure from the ice buckled the wooden planks. Freezing water rushed in, exacerbating the situation — in other words, adding fuel to the fire. Shackleton was forced to order his crew to abandon the ship.

For five months, the weary crew camped on the ice, surviving on penguins and seals. Ice and bad weather continued to hinder any attempt to escape. Finally, the ice broke up enough for them to sail in lifeboats to the uninhabited Elephant Island. From there, Shackleton made a daring attempt: he sailed 800 miles across freezing seas to find help, driven by extraordinary courage and perseverance.

Despite months of fatigue and danger, all 28 members of the expedition survived. The Endurance, however, was condemned to the ocean floor — lost for over a century until its rediscovery in 2022.

中譯重點：1914 年，沙克頓爵士踏上一段傳奇的南極航程。他的目標是率領船員完成首次橫越南極洲的陸地穿越。然而，船隻遭遇浮冰後，被完全困住，整整漂流九個月。冰塊逐漸壓彎木板，海水湧入使情況更加惡化——猶如火上加油。沙克頓被迫命令船員棄船。
五個月後，疲憊的探險隊員在冰上露營，靠企鵝與海豹存活。各種阻礙妨礙了逃脫的計畫。冰層終於裂開，他們乘救生艇抵達無人居住的象島。沙克頓隨後展開一次大膽的嘗試：憑藉超凡的勇氣與毅力，航行 800 英里前往求援。儘管歷經疲勞與危險，全部 28 名隊員均倖存下來。

👆 點此查看答案 ＆ 解析▼
1. (C) trapped　被困住的 — "completely trapped in pack ice" 是 115 閱讀原句。condemned = 譴責；hindered = 妨礙；exacerbated = 使惡化，語意均不符。

2. (C) exacerbated　使惡化 — "add fuel to the fire" 是 exacerbate 的同義慣用語（Q36 錯題核心）。abandoned = 遺棄；rescued = 救援；drifted = 漂流，語意不符。

3. (A) expedition　探險隊 — "expedition team" 是 115 閱讀最後一段原句。vessel = 船隻；fatigue = 疲勞；voyage = 航程，均為名詞但搭配不對。

4. (C) Added fuel to the fire　火上加油 — exacerbate 語意 = 使惡化 = 火上加油（Q36 正解）。Broke the ice = 打破僵局（璇選 A 的錯誤）。

5. (A) Another crucial factor is a society's immigration diversity.　後句講「移民」，空格必須引出「移民」主題 → A 完美銜接（Q33 錯題解析）。選 B 則後句出現邏輯跳躍。

6. (B) The clockwise design favored right-handed defenders.　原文明確說 defenders 熟悉階梯，能 swiftly retreat；A 說 "too narrow to allow any quick retreat" 與原文矛盾（Q42 錯題）。
