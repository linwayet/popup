🔴 不熟悉
① nausea [ˈnɔʒə] n.
噁心感；作嘔；極度厭惡
衍生詞nauseous (adj. 感到噁心的)；nauseating (adj. 令人作嘔的)；nauseate (v. 使感到噁心)
片語nausea and vomiting 噁心嘔吐；suffer from nausea 受噁心之苦；motion-induced nausea 暈動症引起的噁心
容易混淆nausea 是名詞（feel nausea）；nauseous 是形容詞（I feel nauseous 我覺得想吐）；nauseating 是「令人噁心」（the smell is nauseating）
①Motion sickness can trigger severe nausea, especially when reading in a moving vehicle.
暈車症可能引發嚴重的噁心感，尤其是在行進中的交通工具裡看書時。
②The patient experienced nausea and dizziness as the first symptoms of the illness.
病人以噁心和頭暈作為這種疾病的最初症狀。
③The smell of the chemical was so nauseating that workers had to leave the room immediately.
那股化學品的氣味令人作嘔，工作人員不得不立刻離開房間。
🔴 不熟悉
② subsequent [ˈsʌbsɪkwənt] adj.
隨後的；後續的；接下來的
衍生詞subsequently (adv. 隨後；之後)；subsequence (n. 後續事件)
字根sequ / sec 跟隨 → sequence 順序；consequence 結果；sequel 續集；consecutive 連續的
片語subsequent events 後續事件；in subsequent years 在之後幾年；subsequent to 在……之後（正式用語）
容易混淆subsequent 強調時間上緊接其後；following 較口語化；later 更一般；consequent 強調因果關係上的後果
①The initial impact of the earthquake was severe, and the subsequent aftershocks caused further damage.
地震最初的衝擊相當嚴重，而隨後的餘震又造成了更多的損害。
②The scientist's first experiment failed, but subsequent trials led to a breakthrough discovery.
科學家的第一次實驗失敗了，但後續的嘗試卻帶來了突破性的發現。
③Subsequently, the research team detected a pattern in the data that had been overlooked before.
隨後，研究團隊在先前被忽視的數據中偵測到了一個規律。
🔴 不熟悉
③ acoustic [əˈkustɪk] adj.
聲音的；音響的；聽覺相關的
衍生詞acoustics (n. 音響學；音響效果)；acoustically (adv. 在音響方面)
片語acoustic quality 音質；acoustic signal 聲學信號；acoustic guitar 原聲吉他；acoustic chamber 音響腔室
容易混淆acoustic 與聲音效果或音響相關；auditory 指耳朵的「聽覺」感官功能；sonic 指「音速」或「聲波」特性
①The old bells were replaced because their acoustic quality had deteriorated significantly over the centuries.
舊鐘因為幾個世紀以來音質顯著劣化，最終被替換掉了。
②The shrimp creates a powerful acoustic shock wave by snapping its claw at high speed.
這種蝦子以極高的速度夾緊鉗子，產生強大的聲波衝擊。
③Engineers designed the concert hall to have perfect acoustics so that every note could be heard clearly.
工程師設計這座音樂廳時，力求完美的音響效果，讓每個音符都能清晰聽見。

④ trigger [ˈtrɪɡər] v. / n.
觸發；引發；啟動 / 扳機；導火線
衍生詞triggered (adj. 被觸發的)；hair-trigger (adj. 一觸即發的)；triggering (v./adj. 觸發中的)
片語trigger a reaction 觸發反應；trigger a memory 喚起記憶；pull the trigger 扣扳機；trigger point 觸發點
容易混淆trigger 強調引發連鎖反應的起點；cause 較廣義的「造成」；provoke 偏向激怒或引起情緒反應
①Reading in a car can trigger motion sickness in people with a sensitive inner ear.
對於內耳較敏感的人來說，在車裡閱讀可能會觸發暈車反應。

突然的巨大聲響觸發了壓力反應，使那隻動物僵在原地。
③Scientists are studying what triggers the shrimp's snap, since it happens faster than the eye can see.
科學家正在研究是什麼觸發了這種蝦子的夾擊動作，因為它發生的速度快到肉眼無法捕捉。

⑤ symptom [ˈsɪmptəm] n.
症狀；徵兆；表徵
衍生詞symptomatic (adj. 有症狀的；症狀性的；徵兆的)；symptom-free (adj. 無症狀的)
片語common symptoms 常見症狀；display symptoms 出現症狀；symptom of a larger problem 更大問題的徵兆；alleviate symptoms 緩解症狀
容易混淆symptom 指疾病的具體表徵；sign 較廣義的「跡象」（包含醫學和非醫學）；indication 泛指任何暗示或提示
①The most common symptoms of motion sickness are nausea, dizziness, and cold sweats.
暈車症最常見的症狀是噁心感、頭暈和冷汗。
②If you experience any of these symptoms, you should consult a doctor as soon as possible.
如果你出現任何這些症狀，應該盡快去看醫生。
③High unemployment is often seen as a symptom of a deeper economic problem.
高失業率常常被視為更深層經濟問題的徵兆。

⑥ chamber [ˈtʃeɪmbər] n.
腔室；房間；會議廳；議院
衍生詞chambered (adj. 有腔室的)；chamber music 室內樂；single-chamber 單腔的
片語air chamber 氣室；chamber of commerce 商會；inner chamber 內室；gas chamber 毒氣室
容易混淆chamber 強調密閉空間（科學、建築）；room 是一般房間；cavity 指體內的空腔；compartment 指特定隔間
①The shrimp's claw strikes a small water-filled chamber, creating a powerful shock wave.
蝦子的鉗子撞擊一個充滿水的小腔室，產生強大的衝擊波。
②The scientist placed the bacteria in a sealed chamber to observe how they responded to heat.
科學家將細菌放入密封腔室中，觀察它們如何對熱度做出反應。

國會的下議院投票通過了新的環保法案。

偵測；發現；察覺（難以察覺的事物）
衍生詞detection (n. 偵測；發現)；detector (n. 偵測器)；detective (n. 偵探)；detectable (adj. 可偵測的)
片語detect a signal 偵測信號；detect danger 察覺危險；early detection 早期發現；smoke detector 煙霧偵測器

倒塌；崩潰；昏倒 / 倒塌；崩潰

📐Part 2｜文法加強 3 大重點
Day 2 文法來自 Alice 兩次考試的高頻錯題 · 關鍵字粗體標示 · 英中對照
① would + V（過去未來式）vs would have + V-pp（假設語氣）

錯誤來源：114 學測 Q11 — Alice 選 would have spread，正解是 would spread

✅ 站在過去看未來（事情真的發生了）：would + V
✅ 假設與過去相反（事情沒有發生）：would have + V-pp
口訣：「已發生的未來 → would V；沒發生的遺憾 → would have V-pp」
📌 判斷法：問自己「這件事最後有沒有真的發生？」
・In 1995, no one knew it would spread worldwide.（後來真的擴散了 → would spread）
・If she had left earlier, she would have caught the train.（她沒有早點走，沒趕上 → would have caught）
In 1990, scientists discovered a new mechanism that would transform the field of medicine forever.
1990 年，科學家發現了一種新機制，後來確實永遠改變了醫學領域。（真的發生 → would + V）
If researchers had detected the signal earlier, they would have prevented the structural collapse.
如果研究人員早一點偵測到這個信號，他們就能阻止那次結構倒塌了。（沒早點發現 → would have + pp）
When the lab was founded in 1980, no one imagined it would become one of the world's top research centers.
當這間實驗室於 1980 年創立時，沒有人想到它後來真的成為了全球頂尖研究中心。（真的成為了 → would become）
② 介系詞 + whom（關係代名詞受格）

錯誤來源：高二段考 Q23 — Alice 選 who，正解是 whom（of 後面接受格）

✅ 介系詞（of / to / with / for / from…）+ whom（受格）
✅ 主格位置（做主詞）→ who
✅ 受詞位置（做受詞）→ whom（較正式）或 who（口語）
📌 快速判斷：把關係子句還原為一般句，看空格位置是「誰在做主詞」還是「被誰影響的受詞」。
例：400,000 Jews, many of whom died... → of + 受格 → whom
例：The scientist who discovered this... → 主詞位置 → who
The research team had ten members, most of whom had studied acoustic science for over a decade.
研究團隊有十名成員，其中大多數人研究聲學超過十年了。（of + 受格 = whom）
The professor who discovered the mechanism received the Nobel Prize in Physics.
那位發現該機制的教授榮獲了諾貝爾物理學獎。（主詞位置 = who）
She is the scientist whom the committee selected to lead the new detection project.
她就是委員會選定來領導新偵測計畫的那位科學家。（selected 的受詞 = whom）
③ while 的三種核心用法：①同時 ②對比 ③讓步

學測文章高頻字，常出現在文意選填與閱讀測驗中，需正確判斷語意

① 同時（兩件事同時發生）：S + V, while + S + V
② 對比（兩者不同）：S + V, while + S + V （語意上形成反差）
③ 讓步（儘管）：While + S + V, S + V （= although，放句首）
📌 判斷技巧：看兩個子句是否能「同時發生」→ ①；看兩個子句是否「相反對比」→ ②；看 while 子句是否是「讓步條件」→ ③。
①同時：The shrimp snaps its claw while its body remains perfectly stationary.
蝦子夾擊鉗子的同時，身體保持完全靜止不動。
②對比：The large bell survived the revolution, while all the others were melted down for weapons.
那口大鐘在革命中倖存下來，而其他所有的鐘卻都被熔做武器。（對比）
③讓步：While the symptoms of motion sickness may seem mild, they can impact daily life significantly.
儘管暈車症的症狀看起來可能輕微，卻可能對日常生活造成相當大的影響。（讓步 = although）

Nature's Tiny Weapon
~ 115 words · 約學測閱讀 1/2 長度 ~

The snapping shrimp, also known as the pistol shrimp, is one of nature's most remarkable hunters. Despite measuring only a few centimeters, this tiny creature has developed an extraordinary mechanism for catching prey. When it snaps its oversized claw, it creates a powerful acoustic shock wave that can stun or even kill small fish.

The process begins when a bump inside the claw triggers a rapid collapse of a small water-filled chamber. This releases a burst of energy that scientists can detect from a distance. The subsequent pressure wave hits prey with surprising force.

The impact of this discovery has been significant. Engineers are now studying the shrimp's hunting technique to design new underwater technologies.

1. What is the main purpose of the passage?
  • To warn readers about the dangers of snapping shrimp.
  • To explain how the snapping shrimp hunts using sound.
  • To compare the snapping shrimp with other sea creatures.
  • To discuss the negative impact of shrimp on coral reefs.
點此查看答案 ▼
✔ 正解：(B)
全文說明槍蝦如何用聲波狩獵的機制，最後一段才提到工程應用。(A)(C)(D) 都是干擾選項，文章完全沒有涉及危險警告、物種比較或珊瑚礁影響。
2. According to the passage, what triggers the acoustic shock wave?
  • The shrimp's tail hitting the seafloor.
  • A rapid collapse of a water-filled chamber inside the claw.
  • The shrimp releasing a cloud of ink into the water.
  • A loud call that the shrimp produces with its voice.
點此查看答案 ▼
✔ 正解：(B)
第二段明確說明：「a bump inside the claw triggers a rapid collapse of a small water-filled chamber.」直接定位回文找關鍵句，排除其他無中生有選項。
3. The word "subsequent" in paragraph 2 is closest in meaning to:
  • original and unexpected
  • following and coming after
  • powerful and unstoppable
  • gentle and gradual
點此查看答案 ▼
✔ 正解：(B)
subsequent = 隨後的；後來的，closest to "following and coming after"。在文中指「接下來的壓力波」。注意不要選 (C)「強大」— 強大是形容衝擊效果，不是 subsequent 的定義。
✏️Part 4｜單字單選複習 5 題
考驗 Day 2 單字掌握程度 · 完成後按下方按鈕一次查看全部答案與解析
1. In 1968, few scientists imagined that this tiny shrimp ______ inspire a new generation of underwater technology.
  • would have inspired
  • would inspire
  • had inspired
  • has inspired
2. The research team had six members, two of ______ had studied marine acoustics for over twenty years.
  • which
  • that
  • who
  • whom
3. The patient suffered from severe ______ and dizziness after the surgery, so she was kept in the hospital overnight.
  • mechanism
  • nausea
  • chamber
  • impact
4. The shrimp's ______ for hunting is remarkable — it stuns prey using sound rather than physical force.
  • symptom
  • collapse
  • mechanism
  • trigger
5. The ______ tests showed that the treatment was working, as all the initial symptoms had disappeared.
  • subsequent
  • acoustic
  • impactful
  • collapsible
📋 點此查看答案 & 解析  ｜  手機版請調正 ⌄
第 1 題 ✔ 正解：(B) would inspire
站在 1968 年看未來「後來真的啟發了新技術」→ 站在過去看未來的事實 → would + V（過去未來式）。
❌ (A) would have inspired = 假設語氣（「如果…就會啟發了」暗示沒發生，但這裡說的是真的發生的事）。
這正是學測 Q11 的考點！
第 2 題 ✔ 正解：(D) whom
of + 受格 → whom。介系詞 of 後面必須接受格，先行詞是人（members）。
❌ (C) who = 主格，不可放在介系詞後面。
❌ (A) which = 用於事物，不用於人。
第 3 題 ✔ 正解：(B) nausea
nausea = 噁心感（名詞）。搭配 dizziness「頭暈」和術後反應，情境完全符合。
❌ (A) mechanism 是機制，❌ (C) chamber 是腔室，❌ (D) impact 是影響，均不符合身體症狀的情境。
第 4 題 ✔ 正解：(C) mechanism
mechanism = 機制；作用方式。「狩獵機制」= hunting mechanism，這是學術文章的典型搭配。
❌ (D) trigger 是動詞「觸發」，當名詞是「扳機」，不能說 hunting trigger。
第 5 題 ✔ 正解：(A) subsequent
subsequent tests = 後續的測試（在第一次測試之後進行的）。語境：初始症狀消失後的後續追蹤。
❌ (B) acoustic 是「聲音的」，❌ (C) impactful 是「有影響力的」，❌ (D) collapsible 是「可折疊的」，均不符合語境。
