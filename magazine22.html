How Sleep Positions Affect Health

Sleep is a vital natural process that allows your mind and body to recover.

During sleep, your body conserves energy while cells repair themselves.

The position you sleep in plays an important role in maximizing these benefits.

Most people tend to settle into one of several common sleep positions,
each with its own advantages and disadvantages.

Sleeping on your back is considered one of the healthiest options
because it helps reduce joint pressure and supports proper spinal alignment,
which may benefit people with neck or lower back pain.

However, this position can worsen snoring and sleep apnea,
as gravity allows the tongue and soft tissues in the throat to collapse backward,
partially blocking the airway.

For this reason, back sleeping may not be suitable for everyone,
especially those with back pain.

Breathing Issues During Sleep

Stomach sleeping is generally believed to offer the least support for your body.

While it may reduce snoring in certain individuals by keeping airways more open,
this minor benefit comes with many problems.

Stomach sleeping forces your neck to twist to one side for extended periods,
which places stress on your spine and leads to back pain, muscle strain, and joint discomfort.

Additionally, breathing may become restricted,
due to pressure on your chest.

Studies suggest that most people prefer sleeping on their side.

Side sleeping promotes better spinal alignment and is associated with less snoring than back or stomach sleeping.

However, whether you sleep on your left or right side can make a difference.

Left side sleeping is often advised because it reduces acid reflux.

Since your stomach is on the left side of your body, gravity helps keep stomach acid from flowing back into your esophagus.

This position also supports kidney function and lymphatic drainage.

It's particularly helpful for pregnant women as it improves blood flow to the baby and may lessen swelling.

However, people with certain heart conditions may find this position uncomfortable and should consult their doctor.

Right side sleeping may be more comfortable for individuals with specific heart conditions as it places less stress on the heart.

Others report that it simplifies the process of sleeping.

Right side sleeping may be more comfortable for individuals with specific heart conditions as it places less stress on the heart.

文法解析

Grammar Focus

一、原因副詞子句 because / since / as

【引句 Quote】
Since your stomach is on the left side of your body, gravity helps keep stomach acid from flowing back into your esophagus.
【句型】
Since/Because/As + S + V, S + V...
【核心說明】
原因副詞子句用來說明主要子句發生的原因。because 語氣最直接，since 常表示說話者認為原因已知或可理解，as 則較正式且語氣較弱。
【使用規則】
  • 原因子句放句首時，後面通常加逗號。
  • because 後面接完整子句，because of 後面接名詞或 V-ing。
  • since 可表示「自從」或「由於」，要從上下文判斷。
  • 不要在 because/since 子句後再加 so 連接主要子句。
【主題例句】
Because side sleeping supports spinal alignment, many people find it comfortable.
（因為側睡支撐脊椎排列，許多人覺得它很舒服。）
【學習提醒】
中文常說「因為…所以…」，但英文通常不寫 Because..., so...。because 已經負責連接原因與結果，因此主要子句不需要再加 so。
【⚠️ 常見錯誤 / 🎯 精選試題】
✗ Because side sleeping reduces reflux, so it is often advised.
✓ Because side sleeping reduces reflux, it is often advised.（because 已有連接功能，不加 so。）

選擇：____ your stomach is on the left side, gravity may reduce reflux.
(A) Since (B) Although (C) Unless
答案：A

二、形容詞子句 who / which / that

【引句 Quote】
Sleep is a vital natural process that allows your mind and body to recover.
【句型】
N + who/which/that + V...
【核心說明】
形容詞子句用來修飾前面的名詞，補充說明名詞的身分、功能或特徵。本句 that allows... 修飾 process，說明「睡眠」這個過程能讓心智與身體恢復。
【使用規則】
  • who 通常修飾人，which 修飾事物或動物。
  • that 可修飾人或事物，但不用於逗號隔開的非限定子句。
  • 關係代名詞若在子句中作受詞，有時可以省略。
  • 若前面有逗號補充說明，通常使用 which 或 who，不用 that。
【主題例句】
A sleep position that keeps the airway open may reduce snoring.
（能保持呼吸道暢通的睡姿可能減少打鼾。）
【學習提醒】
that 引導形容詞子句時，前面必須有它修飾的名詞。不要把 every sentence with that 都當成名詞子句；先找 that 前面是否有名詞。
【⚠️ 常見錯誤 / 🎯 精選試題】
✗ Sleep is a process allows the body to recover.
✓ Sleep is a process that allows the body to recover.（process 後需要關係代名詞 that 連接子句。）

選擇：The position ____ supports spinal alignment may reduce pain.
(A) who (B) that (C) where
答案：B

三、分詞構句：補充結果

【引句 Quote】
Gravity allows the tongue and soft tissues in the throat to collapse backward, partially blocking the airway.
【句型】
S + V..., V-ing...
【核心說明】
現在分詞構句可用來補充主句造成的結果。partially blocking the airway 說明前面「舌頭與軟組織向後塌陷」造成的後續影響，也就是部分阻塞呼吸道。
【使用規則】
  • 現在分詞 V-ing 常表示主動、同時發生或自然結果。
  • 分詞構句可放在句尾，用逗號與主要子句隔開。
  • 句尾 V-ing 若表示結果，中文常翻成「因而…」。
  • 若邏輯主詞不清楚，應改寫成完整子句避免誤解。
【主題例句】
The pillow pushed his neck forward, causing muscle strain.
（枕頭把他的脖子往前推，因而造成肌肉拉傷。）
【學習提醒】
分詞構句不是隨便把動詞加 ing。要先確認它和主句之間有明確的時間、原因、條件或結果關係；若關係不清楚，寫完整子句會更安全。
【⚠️ 常見錯誤 / 🎯 精選試題】
✗ Gravity pulls tissues backward, block the airway.
✓ Gravity pulls tissues backward, blocking the airway.（句尾補充結果時，用現在分詞 blocking。）

改錯：Stomach sleeping twists the neck, causes strain.
答案：causes → causing

• play an important role in（在…扮演重要角色）

• sleep on one's back/side/stomach（仰睡／側睡／趴睡）

練文筆食材⌄
① allow + O + to V（讓…能夠…）
→ 適用：說明某事物的功能
例：Sleep allows the brain to organize information.
② play an important role in + N/V-ing（在…扮演重要角色）
→ 適用：寫原因或重要性
例：Daily habits play an important role in maintaining health.
③ tend to + V（傾向於…）
→ 適用：描述普遍習慣
例：Teenagers tend to stay up late before exams.
④ each with its own...（每個都有自己的…）
→ 適用：比較不同選項
例：Different study methods exist, each with its own strengths.
⑤ be considered one of...（被認為是…之一）
→ 適用：介紹評價或普遍看法
例：Reading is considered one of the best ways to build vocabulary.
⑥ because + S + V（因為…）
→ 適用：清楚說明原因
例：Because sleep affects memory, students should avoid staying up late.
⑦ which may benefit...（這可能有益於…）
→ 適用：補充前句效果
例：The program offers weekly feedback, which may benefit slow learners.
⑧ as + S + V（因為…）
→ 適用：補充較正式原因
例：This method works well, as it reduces unnecessary pressure.
⑨ V-ing 分詞構句表結果
→ 適用：說明自然後果
例：The bad posture pushed his neck forward, causing pain.
⑩ While + S + V, S + V（雖然…但是…）
→ 適用：呈現優缺點對比
例：While online learning is convenient, it requires self-control.
⑪ by + V-ing（藉由…）
→ 適用：說明方法
例：Students can reduce stress by planning their schedule.
⑫ whether A or B（無論 A 或 B；是 A 還是 B）
→ 適用：比較兩種選擇
例：Whether you study at home or school can affect your focus.
⑬ keep + O + from V-ing（阻止…做某事）
→ 適用：說明防止問題
例：A clear schedule keeps students from wasting time.
⑭ be associated with（與…有關）
→ 適用：描述研究發現
例：Regular exercise is associated with better sleep quality.
⑮ should + V（應該…）
→ 適用：提出溫和建議
例：People with serious symptoms should consult a doctor.
