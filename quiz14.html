The Bioengineering Logic of Balancing Strength and Weight

Original Article

Why are bird skeletons hollow?

Bird skeletons are often described as “hollow,” but this does not mean they are empty or weak. Many bird bones are pneumatized, which means they contain air spaces connected to the lungs, instead of being filled with heavy marrow. These internal spaces help reduce overall body weight while still keeping the skeleton strong enough to support the powerful wing strokes needed for flight.

From a bioengineering point of view, this design is similar to lightweight structures in airplanes or bridges. Engineers know that moving material from the center of a beam to the outside makes it stiffer and stronger, even if the inside is partly hollow. Bird bones follow the same logic: their walls are thin but dense, and many long bones have internal struts like tiny pillars, giving them a high strength‑to‑weight ratio. As a result, bird skeletons can look thin and delicate, but, kilogram for kilogram, they are as heavy as or even denser than the skeletons of many mammals.

These special bones also show the close relationship between structure and the evolution of flight. Over tens of millions of years, natural selection favored birds whose skeletons saved weight yet stayed stiff under bending and twisting forces during flight. At the same time, connecting air-filled bones to the respiratory system improved oxygen exchange, which is vital for long, energy‑hungry flights. In this way, bird skeletons are a good model for high school students to understand how evolution and bioengineering both try to solve the same problem: how to balance strength and weight for efficient movement in the air.

文法解析

Grammar Focus
一、非限定關係子句：which 補充整句資訊
【引句 Quote】
Many bird bones are pneumatized, which means they contain air spaces connected to the lungs.
【句型】

S + V ..., which + V ...

【核心說明】

非限定關係子句通常用逗號隔開，用來補充前面名詞、片語，或整個句子的資訊。在本句中，which means ... 不是限制某一種 bones，而是補充說明 pneumatized 的意思。

這種句型很適合科普文章，因為作者常需要先提出專有名詞，再立刻用 which 解釋概念。

【使用規則】
  • 非限定關係子句前通常有逗號。
  • which 可以指前面的單一名詞，也可以指前面整件事。
  • 非限定用法不能用 that 取代 which。
  • 翻譯時常譯為「這表示……」「而這……」。
【主題例句】

Bird bones contain air spaces, which help reduce body weight.
（鳥骨含有空氣空間，而這有助於減輕體重。）

【學習提醒】

看到逗號加 which 時，不要急著往前只找一個名詞；它常常是在說明前面整個句子的結果或意義。

【⚠️ 常見錯誤 / 🎯 精選試題】

✗ Many bird bones are pneumatized, that means they contain air spaces.
✓ Many bird bones are pneumatized, which means they contain air spaces.（非限定關係子句不能用 that。）

選擇：Bird bones are light, ___ helps them fly efficiently.
(A) that (B) which (C) what
答案：B

二、動名詞片語當主詞
【引句 Quote】
Connecting air-filled bones to the respiratory system improved oxygen exchange.
【句型】

V-ing phrase + V + O

【核心說明】

動名詞片語可以放在句首當主詞，表示一個動作、做法或概念。本句的主詞不是 bones，而是 Connecting air-filled bones to the respiratory system 這整件事。

英文科普寫作常用動名詞主詞來描述「某種設計」或「某個過程」帶來的效果。

【使用規則】
  • 動名詞片語當主詞時，動詞通常用單數。
  • 動名詞強調「這件事」或「這種做法」。
  • 不要把句首 V-ing 誤判成現在進行式。
  • 若主詞太長，可改用 It is ... to V 句型。
【主題例句】

Reducing unnecessary weight makes flight easier.
（減少不必要的重量會讓飛行更容易。）

【學習提醒】

句首看到 V-ing 時，要先判斷它是不是整個句子的主詞；若後面出現單數動詞，很可能就是動名詞片語當主詞。

【⚠️ 常見錯誤 / 🎯 精選試題】

✗ Connect air-filled bones to the respiratory system improves oxygen exchange.
✓ Connecting air-filled bones to the respiratory system improves oxygen exchange.（當主詞需用動名詞。）

改錯：Reduce body weight are important for flight.
答案：Reducing body weight is important for flight.

三、whose 引導形容詞子句
【引句 Quote】
Natural selection favored birds whose skeletons saved weight yet stayed stiff.
【句型】

N + whose + N + V ...

【核心說明】

whose 表示「其……的」，可用來連接人、動物或事物與其擁有的名詞。本句中 whose skeletons 指「那些鳥的骨骼」。

這個句型能把兩個句子合併，讓資訊更緊密：Natural selection favored birds. Their skeletons saved weight.

【使用規則】
  • whose 後面一定接名詞。
  • whose 可用於人，也可用於動物或事物。
  • whose 子句修飾前面的名詞。
  • 不要把 whose 和 who is 混淆。
【主題例句】

Birds whose bones were lighter had an advantage in flight.
（骨頭較輕的鳥類在飛行上具有優勢。）

【學習提醒】

whose 不是 who 的所有格而已，它的功能是帶出「某物屬於前面的名詞」。看到 whose 後面接名詞，就要往前找它修飾的是誰。

【⚠️ 常見錯誤 / 🎯 精選試題】

✗ Natural selection favored birds who skeletons saved weight.
✓ Natural selection favored birds whose skeletons saved weight.（whose 後接名詞 skeletons。）

選擇：Animals ___ bodies are lighter may move faster.
(A) who (B) whose (C) which
答案：B

練文筆食材⌄

① be often described as ...（常被描述為……）
→ 適用：介紹常見觀念或刻板印象時
例：Teenagers are often described as careless, but many of them are highly responsible.

② this does not mean that ...（這並不表示……）
→ 適用：修正誤解、澄清概念
例：Getting a low score does not mean that a student cannot improve.

③ instead of + V-ing/N（而不是……）
→ 適用：表達取代、對比
例：We should solve the problem instead of ignoring it.

④ while still + V-ing（同時仍然……）
→ 適用：強調兼顧兩種目標
例：The new policy saves money while still protecting public safety.

⑤ strong enough to + V（足夠強壯／充分到可以……）
→ 適用：說明能力或條件
例：The bridge is strong enough to survive typhoons.

⑥ from a ... point of view（從……角度來看）
→ 適用：切換分析觀點
例：From an environmental point of view, public transportation is a smart choice.

⑦ be similar to ...（類似於……）
→ 適用：用熟悉事物解釋陌生概念
例：Learning a language is similar to training a muscle.

⑧ even if + S + V（即使……）
→ 適用：讓步與反差
例：Even if the task is difficult, we should not give up too early.

⑨ as a result（因此）
→ 適用：呈現結果
例：The city planted more trees. As a result, the streets became cooler.

⑩ kilogram for kilogram（以每公斤相比）
→ 適用：公平比較不同對象
例：Kilogram for kilogram, this material is stronger than steel.

⑪ over tens of millions of years（在數千萬年間）
→ 適用：描述長期變化
例：Over tens of millions of years, the island developed unique plants.

⑫ whose + N + V（其……的）
→ 適用：合併資訊、描述特徵
例：Students whose study plans are clear usually feel less stressed.

⑬ at the same time（同時）
→ 適用：補充另一個同步發生的重點
例：Technology saves time; at the same time, it creates new challenges.

⑭ be vital for ...（對……至關重要）
→ 適用：強調必要性
例：Clean water is vital for public health.

⑮ how to balance A and B（如何平衡 A 與 B）
→ 適用：討論兩難或設計問題
例：Schools must learn how to balance freedom and discipline.
