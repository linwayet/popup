百科全書的兩千年知識史

2,000 Years of Encyclopedic Knowledge

林威老師與 AI agent 團隊製作

【區塊一】英文文章原文

【原文 Original Article】

Consider the challenge of fitting everything humans know about the world into one place. From facts about unusual plants and animals to distant lands, people have always wanted to gather and preserve knowledge. Encyclopedias were created to do exactly that, turning curiosity into written records and helping others explore the world one page at a time.

This vast work contained 37 books that covered everything from medicine to magic. Pliny's dedication to his work was obvious, yet knowing everything about everything is an impossible task for just one man. Consequently, his texts are filled with wild inaccuracies, such as tales of milk raining from the sky and turtles.

Centuries later, the invention of the printing press made it possible to circulate information even further. In the mid-18th century, Diderot's Encyclopedie, an important French work, was intended to bring valuable knowledge to ordinary people. Meanwhile, in the UK, the Encyclopedia Britannica became hugely popular for its reliable articles written by experts in different fields.

As human knowledge increased, encyclopedia makers struggled to keep pace, with information sometimes becoming outdated as fast as the ink dried.

By 2012, Encyclopedia Britannica had been put online, but it was already late to the party. In 2001, the way we access information changed forever, thanks to the arrival of Wikipedia. Unlike traditional encyclopedias written by experts, Wikipedia allows anyone to contribute and edit articles. The site has been incredibly successful with hundreds of millions of page views daily. However, it also created new challenges, not all information is accurate or reliable, and false information can be added quickly. Perhaps unsurprisingly, some people are turning to AI sources, such as Elon Musk's Grokipedia, which was released in 2025. Grokipedia's entries are written by an AI assistant, not humans. It promises to be closer to the truth, but at the moment, it largely copies Wikipedia entries.

From Pliny to AI assistants via Wikipedia's collaborative platform, encyclopedias have transformed dramatically. When we look to the future, the format will likely continue to change, and so too will the facts. But humanity's hunger for knowledge and our drive to share it? That will never change.

【區塊四】文法解析

【文法解析 Grammar Analysis】

【文法名稱】虛受詞 it 的句型：make it adj. + to V

【引句 Quote】

"Centuries later, the invention of the printing press made it possible to circulate information even further."

【句型】

S + make / find / think / feel + it + adj. / N + to V / that 子句

【核心說明】

當動詞後面的受詞（不定詞片語或名詞子句）太長時，英文習慣使用虛受詞 it 代替真正的受詞，將真受詞往後挪。這能避免句子顯得頭重腳輕，讓受詞補語（形容詞）緊跟在動詞之後。

【使用規則】

  • ① 常見動詞包括 make (使得)、find (發現)、think (認為)、feel (覺得)。
  • ② it 為虛受詞，不需翻譯。
  • ③ 形容詞補語緊接在 it 之後。
  • ④ 真正受詞通常由 to V 或 that 引導的子句組成。

【主題例句】

Wikipedia made it easier for ordinary people to access knowledge.（維基百科使得普通人獲取知識變得更容易。）

【學習提醒】

這種句型中，絕不能省略 it，也不能直接用 to V 放在形容詞前面。例如：made possible to circulate 是錯誤的。

【⚠️ 常見錯誤 / 🎯 精選試題】

✗ I found possible to finish the work in one day.
✓ I found it possible to finish the work in one day.
說明：必須加上虛受詞 it。

選擇題：The new technology makes ________ convenient to shop online. (A) this (B) that (C) it (D) which

答案：C。說明：固定句型 make it adj. to V。

【文法名稱】過去分詞當形容詞片語 (p.p. modifier)

【引句 Quote】

"Unlike traditional encyclopedias written by experts, Wikipedia allows anyone to contribute and edit articles."

【句型】

N + p.p. (過去分詞) ...

【核心說明】

過去分詞 (p.p.) 可以放在名詞後面，用來修飾該名詞，表示「被動」或「完成」的狀態。這通常是形容詞子句（N + who/which + be + p.p.）省略了關係代名詞和 be 動詞後的簡化形式。

【使用規則】

  • ① 修飾的名詞與分詞之間具備被動關係。
  • ② 常見於「被……所撰寫」、「被……所製造」等情境。
  • ③ p.p. 片語後面常伴隨 by 指出行為者。

【主題例句】

Articles written by AI assistants are now appearing online.（由 AI 助手撰寫的文章現在正出現在網路上。）

【學習提醒】

判斷要用 V-ing 或 p.p. 時，看它修飾的名詞。百科全書是被撰寫 (written)，所以用過去分詞。

【⚠️ 常見錯誤 / 🎯 精選試題】

✗ The house building many years ago is still strong.
✓ The house built many years ago is still strong.
說明：房子是被建造，應使用 p.p.。

選擇題：This is a book ________ by a famous author. (A) wrote (B) writing (C) written (D) write

答案：C。說明：書是被撰寫，選 p.p.。

【文法名稱】So too + 倒裝句：表示「也一樣」

【引句 Quote】

"When we look to the future, the format will likely continue to change, and so too will the facts."

【句型】

..., and so too + be/助動詞 + S

【核心說明】

這是一種強調語氣的倒裝句型，用來表示第二個主詞也具備與前方提到的相同情況。其功能類似於 "S + too" 或 "so + be/助動詞 + S"，但語氣較為正式且強烈。

【使用規則】

  • ① 必須先有一個肯定句。
  • ② 倒裝部分的助動詞（如 will, can）或 be 動詞需與前句時態一致。
  • ③ so too 放句首，動詞（助動詞）放在主詞前面。

【主題例句】

As technology evolves, the way we learn changes, and so too does the world.（隨著科技進化，我們學習的方式改變，世界也同樣改變。）

【學習提醒】

注意動詞的一致性。如果前句是 will change，後句就用 will；如果是 changes，後句則用 does。

【⚠️ 常見錯誤 / 🎯 精選試題】

✗ He is happy, and so too I am.
✓ He is happy, and so too am I.
說明：主詞與動詞需倒裝。

選擇題：Knowledge is expanding, and so too ________ our tools. (A) are (B) do (C) have (D) will

答案：A。說明：前句使用 is (be動詞)，後句對應複數主詞 tools 應選 are。

折疊四：練文筆食材 ▼
① fit everything ... into one place（將所有事物整合於一處）
→ 適用：描述整理大量資訊或資源時。
例：The library fits centuries of history into one place.
② turning curiosity into ...（將好奇心轉化為……）
→ 適用：描述將抽象想法變為具體成果時。
例：She succeeded by turning curiosity into innovation.
③ knowing everything about everything is an impossible task（無所不知是不可能的任務）
→ 適用：強調個人能力的限制或謙遜時。
例：Modern science shows that knowing everything about everything is an impossible task.
④ made it possible to ...（使得……成為可能）
→ 適用：描述某項發明或條件帶來的進步。
例：New technology made it possible to work from anywhere.
⑤ keep pace with ...（與……保持同步）
→ 適用：描述在快速變化的環境中不落後。
例：Education must keep pace with the rapid changes in society.
⑥ late to the party（姍姍來遲 / 晚了一步）
→ 適用：描述某個趨勢或機會被錯過時。
例：Many traditional companies were late to the party of online shopping.
⑦ thanks to the arrival of ...（多虧了……的到來）
→ 適用：正面描述某事物的出現帶來的影響。
例：Life became easier thanks to the arrival of smartphones.
⑧ allows anyone to contribute（允許任何人做出貢獻）
→ 適用：描述民主化或開放式的協作系統。
例：The open-source project allows anyone to contribute to the code.
⑨ promises to be closer to the truth（承諾將更接近真相）
→ 適用：描述某種新方法或工具的優勢。
例：The new investigation promises to be closer to the truth than the previous one.
⑩ humanity's hunger for knowledge（人類對知識的渴求）
→ 適用：描述人類進步的原始動力。
例：Humanity's hunger for knowledge drives us to explore space.
